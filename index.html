How this model works

Every headline figure is the same arithmetic: a scope count you enter (homes, accounts, entities, family members, staff), multiplied by an hours-saved-per-unit-per-year figure for each driver. Total hours convert to a dollar equivalent at the loaded rate you set. Nothing is hidden: turn on Show & edit assumptions on the Calculator tab to see and adjust every per-unit number.

We separate two things on purpose, because that distinction is what makes the model defensible rather than a black box:

The problem · sourced

That each driver addresses a real, documented inefficiency in offices like yours. These premises are supported by independent family-office research, cited below.

The magnitude · Eleven estimate

The specific hours-per-unit figures are Eleven's conservative estimates, calibrated with pilot customers. No published study measures hours saved at this granularity; that number only ever comes from real deployments, including yours.

So the right way to read a footnote here is: the research establishes that the problem is real and widespread; the hours figure is our estimate of how much you recover, which we tune to your own before-and-after numbers as a pilot progresses.

What the research supports, by module
Eleven Financial & AI Assistants
Consolidated reporting, document extraction, instant answers
Persistent reliance on spreadsheets and manual data aggregation remains a significant concern across the industry, and only about a third of family offices use leading-edge technology outside of investing. This is the exact manual burden these drivers remove.
AI-assisted monitoring & retrieval
Interest in AI is high with early adoption underway in research, reporting, and risk; the model prices the time this shift gives back.
Eleven Family
Travel & residency tracking, centralized records
57% of family offices have at least one member living outside the office's primary jurisdiction, and supporting them is a high priority for 85%, with tax, investment, and estate planning the most pressing issues. The cross-border complexity these drivers manage is documented, not assumed.
Dollar conversion & the talent lens
Loaded-rate value of recovered hours
Talent is the single largest operating expense for family offices, with 70% reporting difficulty hiring and 65% concerned about retention. When you cannot easily hire or retain, recovered staff hours are not just a cost saving, they are capacity you otherwise cannot buy.
Messages, Security & Vault (soft benefits)
Audit-ready approvals, knowledge preserved, emergency-ready records
Two-thirds of offices lack a formal conflict-resolution mechanism and nearly half operate without a documented succession plan or constitution; staff turnover ranks among the most significant operational risks. These are the documented gaps the qualitative benefits speak to.
A note on positioning

The source report frames outsourcing as the primary answer to these gaps. Eleven's position is that a platform lets an office reach the same operational excellence while keeping data, control, and institutional knowledge in-house, an outcome the research itself values when it notes that three out of four offices believe in-house teams deliver the most timely, highest-quality results. The research is useful for establishing the problem; Eleven is the in-house path to the outcome it recommends.
